CAs need to leverage their expert functional knowledge using technology

Challenges for CAs

When I qualified as a Chartered Accountant in the 70's, 90% of the companies had manual accounting and 10% were on automated systems. I had a choice. Today's world is dominated by technology and nearly 100% of the organisations are running on computerised systems. You don't have a choice; it is absolutely essential to understand the fundamentals of technology and the basics of how data seamlessly flows in an organisation. Most of the young CAs who pass out today have been exposed to small accounting systems during their articleship and when they join large organisations they are suddenly exposed to high end ERP systems. The shift can be very challenging. In the banking world the Core Banking Software can be integrated with many satellite systems. Data is passed on from one system to the other and technology makes it happen. Most of this data has an impact on the financial statements. As CAs we should be in a position to identify the risks and recommend suitable controls to mitigate these risks. Today the words "Governance, Risk & Compliance" are playing an important role worldwide; you should be part of it!

Opportunities

The gruelling training of the CA course has created Chartered Accountants with an excellent understanding of business processes and its impact on the financial statements. From the time of articleship, tracing source documents to financial statements is a daily phenomena. Understanding data flow within or across an organisation is part of the DNA of any CA. This is a very powerful skill set. The world today is looking for Business Process Experts who can use their domain knowledge to make business processes efficient, effective and risk free. I believe that the most logical profession to fill this gap are Chartered Accountants. Organisations would prefer to talk to consultants that see the 'big picture'. Today, many organisations work in two 'silos'; domain & techonology! Communicating across silos is not easy since CAs may talk "debits & credits" while technologists in terms of "Java, .Net, JBoss, Linux, ABAP", etc! There has to be a cross over for things to happen efficiently & effectively. I believe that if CAs go that extra mile in understanding technology the returns to the organisation and the individual can increase manifold!

The Road Ahead

The world has moved from manual -> batch -> online -> real time systems. We are now moving into the world of smart phones; technology will consume us as we go forward. This should not be a deterrent to CAs in carrying out their professional activities. Instead, it is a great opportunity! Use your vast domain knowledge for analytics and let the machines do the number crunching. Data will seamlessly flow across organisations and borders. This will impact financial statements and also have legal implications. We should be aware. The irony of computerised sysytems is that all reports generated look neat, clean and correct. This can be a misnomer. We should understand the risks and vulnerabilites. Ask fundamantal questions pertaining to the activities of 'Input', 'Processing' and 'Output'. The best way to learn is to get your hands wet. Take the plunge and I am sure you will not drown! The rigorous training that all CAs have gone through will always keep your head above the water. Remember the words of wisdom of Mahatma Gandhi: "Be the change you want to see"
